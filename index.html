UI DesignDesign System2023–2025
DataGalaxy — Evolving the Design System

Building and scaling a component library that helped a B2B SaaS team ship faster and with more consistency across the product.

FigmaFigJamStorybookNotionJira
The Problem

DataGalaxy's product had grown fast — different teams built UI independently, causing visual inconsistencies, duplicated effort, and slow onboarding for new designers and developers. There was no single source of truth.

My Role

I audited existing components across 40+ screens, identified redundancies, and helped define the foundational token system — colors, spacing, typography. I built and documented reusable Figma components and collaborated with developers to ensure design–dev parity.

Process

Started with a full UI audit. Grouped components by frequency and complexity. Ran workshops with engineers to align naming conventions. Iterated through weekly design crits, building from atoms to full page templates.

What I Learned

A design system is never finished — it's a living product. The biggest challenge wasn't the components themselves but getting buy-in from the team to use and contribute to it. Communication and documentation mattered as much as design craft.

UX ResearchProduct Design2024
DataGalaxy — Reducing Friction in the Core Workflow

Identifying and resolving key pain points in the web app's primary user journey through research, usability testing, and rapid iteration.

FigmaMazeFigJamNotionHotjar
The Problem

User interviews and support tickets pointed to a recurring theme: users struggled to navigate the data catalog and complete core tasks without assistance. Drop-off in onboarding was high and experienced users relied on workarounds.

My Role

I led the research phase — conducting 6 user interviews and synthesising findings into a journey map. I translated insights into design improvements, ran usability tests, and iterated before dev handoff.

Process

Discovery → affinity mapping → problem framing → ideation workshop → lo-fi wireframes → hi-fi prototypes → unmoderated usability testing with 5 participants → final iteration → annotated dev handoff.

What I Learned

Watching users test is humbling. Assumptions I had made in the design were consistently wrong in the same places. Testing early with even a handful of users saved significant rework downstream.

Maré — Coastal Café Brand Identity

Full visual identity for a Toulon café — from naming and logo to packaging, print collateral, and a complete brand book.

IllustratorPhotoshopInDesign
The Brief

The client wanted a brand that felt warm, premium, and distinctly coastal — standing out in a saturated café market while connecting emotionally with locals in Toulon.

My Role

Sole designer across the full brand — strategy, naming, logo design, color and typography system, packaging, menu design, and brand guidelines document.

Process

Brand audit → moodboarding → 3 logo concepts → client review → refinement → full system rollout across print and digital touchpoints → brand guidelines PDF handoff.

What I Learned

Freelance brand projects taught me how to manage client expectations and translate abstract feelings ("warm but premium") into concrete visual decisions. Presenting rationale clearly is half the design work.
